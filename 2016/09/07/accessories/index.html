Adding Accessories

I know it has been a while since I last posted, but life has been crazy. Here we go!

Steam Link & Controllers

A pretty great deal recently presented itself on these, so I decided to stop being stubborn and dive on in to the world of “streaming media to my TV.” Steam Big Picture provides a pretty intuitive way of accessing my whole Steam library from a controller, and turns everything into a more TV friendly format.

The Steam Link itself acts as a bridge from my gaming PC to my TV.

I have friends who have had lag/responsiveness issues when bridging over wireless connections, but I have not run into any particularly bad issues when connected over an 802.11AC network. However, nothing beats a wired connection, so when I have the necessary infrastructure, I will eventually switch over.

Additionally, the Steam controllers and their touch-sensitive directional pads took some getting used to, but eventually I was able to make the switch from my XBOX 360 controllers.

An interesting quirk about these is that Steam has to be open for them to function, they do not seem to pass commands directly through to the OS. This has caused issues for me when trying to create VOIP keybinds and other hotkeys. I eventually got it all working, but it may prove difficult for someone who relies on plug and play functionality.

Amazon FireTV

I went ahead and moved from the FireTV Stick to the FireTV box due to the increased horsepower, which makes an incredible difference when running software like KODI. In addition, we have the apps for Netflix, HBO Now, and soon, PS Vue or SlingTV (We have not decided yet.)

It also has a voice enabled controller, which allows me to use the controller to speak commands directly to the Echo! This is an incredibly helpful feature, and I was pretty darn excited when I found out about it. I believe that voice controllers can be purchased for FireTV sticks as well.
