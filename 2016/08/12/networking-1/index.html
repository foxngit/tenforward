Networking, Part 1

I have had an awesome experience with Ubiquiti (UBNT) Unifi products in the past, so I decided that I would go with Unifi at home as well. Although the hardware is a bit pricier and requires more knowledge than the alternatives, the reliability, quality, performance, and control more than make up for it.

ArsTechnica has a great writeup on Unifi products here: Ubiquiti UniFi made me realize…

Unifi Security Gateway

This is the Unifi equivalent of the Ubiquiti Edge Router Lite. Same hardware, slightly higher price tag, but integrates seamlessly with the Unifi Management Console. Although UBNT Support has published a feature roadmap and they are continuously developing on this device, I am looking forward to the day when all settings and features are accessible from the Management Page.

Link: Amazon

Unifi UAP

The base model AP that I bought a long time ago. After running for over 3 years, I thought it had been bricked when it became unresponsive. However, a hard reset and re-adoption fixed the issue! I am looking for more modern features now, so I decided to go with the UAP-AC-PRO. However, if you don’t need 802.11AC and you don’t want to spend a lot of time geeking out on the management side of things, a three pack of these can be found for ~$170. Worth it.

Unifi UAP-AC-PRO

Although this was around 3 times more expensive ($170/ea) than the previously listed base model UAP, the functionaliy it provides through the Unifi Management Console is more than worth it! RF Surveys and 802.11AC are the big ones. Seeing all of those greyed out areas in the console become active
